## Scenario: When People Ask Too Much

### Your Goal
Polish people can be curious about foreigners. You'll get questions about where you're from, why you're here, your rent, your job, and your Polish level. Learn to deflect without being rude.

### Setting
Anywhere - elevator, shop queue, neighbor encounters. Someone has decided you're interesting and wants to know more than you want to share.

---

## Question 1: "Where Are You From?"

**This is THE most common question. Be prepared.**

**Them:** Skąd pan/pani jest? `(skond pan/PA-nee yest)` - Where are you from?
**Them:** Skąd pan/pani? `(skond pan/PA-nee)` - Where are you from? *(shorter)*

**Option A - Answer directly:**
- **You:** Z Ameryki. `(z a-meh-RIH-kee)` - From America.
- **You:** Z Anglii. `(z AN-glee-ee)` - From England.
- **You:** Z Kanady. `(z ka-NA-dih)` - From Canada.

**Option B - Be vague:**
- **You:** Z zagranicy. `(z za-gra-NEE-tsih)` - From abroad.
- *This usually doesn't satisfy them but it's an option*

**Option C - Redirect:**
- **You:** Z daleka. A pan/pani stąd? `(z da-LEH-ka. a pan/PA-nee stond)` - From far away. Are you from here?
- *Turn the question around*

---

## Question 2: "Why Are You in Poland?"

**Follow-up after where you're from.**

**Them:** Dlaczego pan/pani jest w Polsce? `(dla-CHE-go pan/PA-nee yest v POL-stse)` - Why are you in Poland?
**Them:** Co pan/pani tu robi? `(tso pan/PA-nee too RO-bee)` - What do you do here?

**Option A - Simple answer:**
- **You:** Praca. `(PRA-tsa)` - Work.
- **You:** Studiuję. `(stoo-DYOO-yeh)` - I'm studying.
- **You:** Rodzina. `(ro-JEE-na)` - Family.

**Option B - Vague:**
- **You:** Tak wyszło. `(tak VISH-wo)` - It just happened.
- **You:** Lubię Polskę. `(LOO-byeh POL-skeh)` - I like Poland.

**Option C - End the conversation:**
- **You:** Długa historia. `(DWOO-ga hees-TOR-ya)` - Long story.
- *Smile and change subject*

---

## Question 3: "Why Don't You Speak Polish Better?"

**This one can sting. People don't always realize it's rude.**

**Them:** Dlaczego pan/pani nie mówi lepiej po polsku? `(dla-CHE-go pan/PA-nee nyeh MOO-vee LEH-pyey po POL-skoo)` - Why don't you speak Polish better?
**Them:** Ile pan/pani tu mieszka? `(EE-leh pan/PA-nee too MYESH-ka)` - How long have you lived here?
*...implying you should speak better by now*

**Option A - Self-deprecating:**
- **You:** Polski jest trudny! `(POL-skee yest TROOD-nih)` - Polish is hard!
- *This usually gets sympathetic agreement*

**Option B - Deflect:**
- **You:** Uczę się. `(OO-cheh sheh)` - I'm learning.
- **You:** Próbuję. `(proo-BOO-yeh)` - I'm trying.

**Option C - Honest:**
- **You:** Nie mam dużo czasu. `(nyeh mam DOO-zho CHA-soo)` - I don't have much time.

**Option D - Turn it around (advanced):**
- **You:** A pan/pani mówi po [your language]? `(a pan/PA-nee MOO-vee po...)` - Do you speak [your language]?
- *Usually shuts down the criticism*

---

## Question 4: "How Much Is Your Rent?"

**Poles can be direct about money. This can feel intrusive.**

**Them:** Ile płacisz za mieszkanie? `(EE-leh PWA-cheesh za myesh-KA-nyeh)` - How much do you pay for your apartment?
**Them:** Ile kosztuje czynsz? `(EE-leh kosh-TOO-yeh chinsh)` - How much is your rent?

**Option A - Answer vaguely:**
- **You:** Dużo. `(DOO-zho)` - A lot.
- **You:** Za dużo! `(za DOO-zho)` - Too much!

**Option B - Deflect:**
- **You:** Nie pamiętam dokładnie. `(nyeh pa-MYEN-tam dok-WAD-nyeh)` - I don't remember exactly.
- **You:** Normalnie. `(nor-MAL-nyeh)` - Normal [price].

**Option C - Politely refuse:**
- **You:** Wolę nie mówić. `(VO-leh nyeh MOO-veech)` - I prefer not to say.

---

## Question 5: "How Much Do You Earn?"

**Even more intrusive, but some people ask.**

**Them:** Ile zarabiasz? `(EE-leh za-RA-byash)` - How much do you earn?
**Them:** Ile płacą? `(EE-leh PWA-tsoh)` - How much do they pay?

**Option A - Deflect with humor:**
- **You:** Za mało! `(za MA-wo)` - Too little!
- *Everyone can relate to this*

**Option B - Vague:**
- **You:** Normalnie. `(nor-MAL-nyeh)` - Normal.
- **You:** Wystarczająco. `(vih-star-cha-YON-tso)` - Enough.

**Option C - Politely refuse:**
- **You:** To prywatne. `(to prih-VAT-neh)` - That's private.
- **You:** Wolę nie mówić. `(VO-leh nyeh MOO-veech)` - I prefer not to say.

---

## Question 6: "Are You Married? Do You Have Children?"

**Personal questions about family.**

**Them:** Ma pan/pani dzieci? `(ma pan/PA-nee JEH-chee)` - Do you have children?
**Them:** Jest pan żonaty? / Jest pani mężatką? `(yest pan zho-NA-tih / yest PA-nee men-ZHAT-koh)` - Are you married? (m/f)

**Option A - Answer:**
- **You:** Tak. / Nie. `(tak / nyeh)` - Yes. / No.

**Option B - Deflect:**
- **You:** Nie chcę mówić. `(nyeh htse MOO-veech)` - I don't want to talk about it.

**Option C - Redirect:**
- **You:** A pan/pani? `(a pan/PA-nee)` - And you?

## General Deflection Phrases

**When you don't want to answer anything:**

| Polish | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| Nie wiem | nyeh vyem | I don't know |
| Długa historia | DWOO-ga hees-TOR-ya | Long story |
| To skomplikowane | to skom-plee-ko-VA-neh | It's complicated |
| Wolę nie mówić | VO-leh nyeh MOO-veech | I prefer not to say |
| To prywatne | to prih-VAT-neh | That's private |
| Przepraszam, śpieszę się | psheh-PRA-sham, SHPYEH-sheh sheh | Sorry, I'm in a hurry |

---

## Ending the Conversation

**When you need to escape:**

- **You:** Przepraszam, muszę iść. `(psheh-PRA-sham, MOO-sheh eeshch)` - Sorry, I have to go.
- **You:** Miłego dnia! `(mee-WEH-go dnya)` - Have a nice day!
- **You:** Do widzenia! `(do vee-JEN-ya)` - Goodbye!

**Just walk away. It's okay.**

## Cultural Notes

- **Curiosity, not hostility**: Most questions come from genuine curiosity about foreigners, not nosiness. Poland doesn't have as many immigrants as some countries.
- **Direct culture**: Poles tend to be more direct than some cultures. Questions about money aren't as taboo as in English-speaking countries.
- **It's okay to deflect**: "Wolę nie mówić" is perfectly acceptable. You don't owe anyone answers.
- **The Polish question**: Questions about your Polish level are CONSTANT. Have a ready answer.
- **Turn it around**: Asking "A pan/pani?" (And you?) is a great way to redirect.

---

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- "Skąd pan?" → "Z Ameryki"
- Any money question → "Za dużo!" + smile
- Exit: "Przepraszam, muszę iść"

### Challenge Version
- Deflect with "Długa historia"
- Refuse politely: "Wolę nie mówić"
- Redirect: "A pan/pani stąd?"
