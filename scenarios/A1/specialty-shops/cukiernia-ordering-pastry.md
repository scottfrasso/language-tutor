## Scenario: Ordering Pastry at Cukiernia

### Your Goal
You want to buy one pastry (ciasto) from a cukiernia (pastry shop). You'll choose from the display and order it.

### Setting
You're at a local cukiernia. There's a glass display case with various cakes and pastries. A shop attendant is behind the counter.

---

## Dialogue

### Opening

**Attendant:** Dzień dobry! `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day!

**You:** Dzień dobry. `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day.

---

### Ordering

**You:** Poproszę to ciasto. `(po-PRO-sheh to CHAS-to)` - I'd like this pastry, please. *(point to the one you want)*

**Alternative:**
- **You:** Poproszę pączka. `(po-PRO-sheh PONCH-ka)` - I'd like a donut, please.
- **You:** Poproszę kawałek sernika. `(po-PRO-sheh ka-VA-wek ser-NEE-ka)` - I'd like a piece of cheesecake, please.

---

### Attendant's Responses

**Response A - Confirms:**
- **Attendant:** Ten? `(ten)` - This one? *(points)*
- **You:** Tak. `(tak)` - Yes.

**Response B - Asks for clarification:**
- **Attendant:** Który? `(KTOO-rih)` - Which one?
- **You:** Ten. `(ten)` - This one. *(point more clearly)*

**Response C - Suggests:**
- **Attendant:** To jest bardzo dobre! `(to yest BAR-dzo DOB-reh)` - This one is very good!
- **You:** Super, poproszę. `(SOO-per, po-PRO-sheh)` - Great, I'll take it.

---

### Optional: Asking About Filling

**You:** Co jest w środku? `(tso yest v SHROD-koo)` - What's inside?

**Attendant:** Jabłko. `(YAB-wko)` - Apple.
  OR: Krem. `(krem)` - Cream.
  OR: Czekolada. `(che-ko-LA-da)` - Chocolate.

**You:** Dziękuję, to poproszę. `(jen-KOO-yeh, to po-PRO-sheh)` - Thank you, I'll take it.

---

### Payment

**Attendant:** Cztery złote pięćdziesiąt. `(CHTE-rih ZWO-teh pyen-JEH-shont)` - Four złoty fifty.
  OR: Cztery pięćdziesiąt. `(CHTE-rih pyen-JEH-shont)` - Four fifty. *(shortened)*

**You:** Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)` - Here you go. *(hand over money)*

**Attendant:** Dziękuję. `(jen-KOO-yeh)` - Thank you.

---

### Closing

**Attendant:** Smacznego! `(sma-CHNE-go)` - Enjoy!

**You:** Dziękuję, do widzenia! `(jen-KOO-yeh, do vee-JEN-ya)` - Thank you, goodbye!

## Pronunciation Tips

1. **"Ciasto"**: CHAS-to, not "see-asto"
2. **"Pączek"**: PONCH-ek. The "ą" is nasal
3. **"Środku"**: SHROD-koo. The "ś" is soft
4. **"Smacznego"**: sma-CHNE-go. You'll hear this a lot!

---

## Cultural Notes

- **"Smacznego"**: Always said at pastry shops when handing you food
- **Pointing is normal**: Everyone points at what they want through the display case
- **Fresh daily**: Polish pastry shops make fresh pastries daily
- **Popular treat**: Pączki (donuts) are especially popular on Fat Thursday (Tłusty Czwartek)

---

## Common Complications

**What if you want to eat there vs take away?**

- **Attendant:** Na miejscu czy na wynos? `(na MYEYS-tsoo chih na VIH-nos)` - Here or to go?
- **You:** Na wynos. `(na VIH-nos)` - To go.
  OR: Na miejscu. `(na MYEYS-tsoo)` - Here.

**What if you want more than one?**

- **You:** Dwa pączki, poproszę. `(dva PONCH-kee, po-PRO-sheh)` - Two donuts, please.

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- "Dzień dobry"
- "Poproszę to" *(point)*
- "Dziękuję, do widzenia"

- Name the specific pastry: "Poproszę pączka"
- Ask what's inside: "Co jest w środku?"
