## Scenario: Buying Fruit at Farmers Market

### Your Goal
You want to buy fresh apples (jabłka) or tomatoes (pomidory) from a stall at the farmers market (targ).

### Setting
You're at a local farmers market (targ). There are stalls with fruits and vegetables. You approach a vendor selling apples.

---

## Dialogue

### Opening

**You approach the stall:**

**Vendor:** Dzień dobry! `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day!

**You:** Dzień dobry. `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day.

**Alternative opening:**
- **Vendor:** Poproszę! `(po-PRO-sheh)` - Yes? / What would you like?
- **You:** Poproszę jabłka. `(po-PRO-sheh YAB-wka)` - I'd like apples, please.

---

### Asking for Fruit

**You:** Poproszę jabłka. `(po-PRO-sheh YAB-wka)` - I'd like apples, please.

**Alternative requests:**
- **You:** Poproszę pomidory. `(po-PRO-sheh po-mee-DO-rih)` - I'd like tomatoes, please.
- **You:** Poproszę truskawki. `(po-PRO-sheh troos-KAF-kee)` - I'd like strawberries, please.

---

### Vendor's Responses

**Response A - How much:**
- **Vendor:** Ile? `(EE-leh)` - How much? / How many?
- **You:** Kilogram. `(kee-lo-GRAM)` - A kilogram.
  OR: Pół kilo. `(poow KEE-lo)` - Half a kilo.
  OR: Dwa kilo. `(dva KEE-lo)` - Two kilos.

**Response B - Points to scale:**
- **Vendor:** *Starts putting apples in bag and weighing*
- **Vendor:** Ile? Tyle? `(EE-leh? TIH-leh)` - How much? This much?
- **You:** Tak. `(tak)` - Yes.
  OR: Więcej. `(VYEN-tsey)` - More.
  OR: Mniej. `(mnyey)` - Less.

**Response C - Offers sample:**
- **Vendor:** Proszę spróbować! `(PRO-sheh SPROO-bo-vatch)` - Try one!
- **You:** Dziękuję. `(jen-KOO-yeh)` - Thank you. *(taste it)*
- **You:** Dobre! Poproszę kilogram. `(DOB-reh! po-PRO-sheh kee-lo-GRAM)` - Good! I'll take a kilogram.

---

### Price

**Vendor:** Osiem złotych. `(O-shem ZWO-tih)` - Eight złoty.
  OR: Osiem. `(O-shem)` - Eight.

**You:** Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)` - Here you go. *(hand over money)*

**Vendor:** Dziękuję! `(jen-KOO-yeh)` - Thank you!

**If you get change:**
- **Vendor:** I dwa złote reszty. `(ee dva ZWO-teh RESH-tih)` - And two złoty change.

---

### Closing

**Vendor:** Dziękuję, do widzenia! `(jen-KOO-yeh, do vee-JEN-ya)` - Thank you, goodbye!

**You:** Do widzenia! `(do vee-JEN-ya)` - Goodbye!

**Alternative:**
- **Vendor:** Smacznego! `(sma-CHNE-go)` - Enjoy!
- **You:** Dziękuję! `(jen-KOO-yeh)` - Thank you!

## Pronunciation Tips

1. **"Jabłka"**: YAB-wka. The "ł" sounds like "w"
2. **"Pomidory"**: po-mee-DO-rih. Stress on DO
3. **"Kilogram"**: kee-lo-GRAM. Can be shortened to "kilo"
4. **"Więcej"**: VYEN-tsey. The "ię" is nasal
5. **"Mniej"**: mnyey. Short and quick

## Cultural Notes

- **Bargaining**: Unlike supermarkets, some market vendors are open to bargaining, especially at the end of the day. But as a beginner, just accept their price - it's already cheap!
- **Bring your own bag**: Most vendors don't provide bags. Bring your own reusable bag.
- **Fresh and local**: Farmers market produce is usually fresher and cheaper than supermarkets.
- **Seasonal**: Selection varies by season. Summer has the most variety.
- **Cash only**: Most market vendors only accept cash (gotówka), not cards.
- **Friendly vendors**: Market vendors are usually friendly and happy to help you practice Polish!

## Common Complications

**What if you want to pick your own?**

- **You:** Mogę wybrać? `(MO-geh VIH-brach)` - Can I choose?
- **Vendor:** Proszę! `(PRO-sheh)` - Go ahead!
  OR: *Vendor picks for you - this is normal*

**What if the price is per item, not per kilo?**

- **Vendor:** Złoty za sztukę. `(ZWO-tih za SHTOO-keh)` - One złoty per piece.
- **You:** Pięć sztuk, poproszę. `(pyench shtook, po-PRO-sheh)` - Five pieces, please.

**What if they try to upsell?**

- **Vendor:** Może jeszcze truskawki? `(MO-zheh YESH-cheh troos-KAF-kee)` - Maybe also strawberries?
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. Tylko jabłka. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh. TIL-ko YAB-wka)` - No, thank you. Only apples.

---

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- "Dzień dobry"
- "Poproszę jabłka" *(hold up one finger for "kilo")*
- Pay and say "Dziękuję"

### Challenge Version
- Specify amount: "Pół kilo, poproszę"
- If they put too much: "Mniej, proszę"
- Try bargaining: "To za drogo" (That's too expensive) - but only if you're confident!
