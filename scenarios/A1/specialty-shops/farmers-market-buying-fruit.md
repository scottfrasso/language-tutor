## Scenario: Buying Fruit When Things Get Confusing

### Your Goal
You want to buy fruit at the farmers market (targ). But: the vendor might pick bad fruit for you, the pricing might be confusing (per kilo vs per piece), or they might add extras you didn't ask for.

### Setting
You're at a farmers market. There are stalls with fruits and vegetables. Vendors are calling out to customers. It's a bit chaotic. Cash only.

---

## The Basic Request

**You:** Dzień dobry. Poproszę jabłka. `(jen DOB-rih. po-PRO-sheh YAB-wka)` - Good day. I'd like apples.

**Specify amount:**
- Kilogram, poproszę. `(kee-lo-GRAM, po-PRO-sheh)` - One kilo, please.
- Pół kilo. `(poow KEE-lo)` - Half a kilo.

---

## Complication 1: The Vendor Picks Bad Fruit

**The vendor is picking fruit for you, and you see they're choosing overripe or bruised ones.**

**Your options:**

**Option A - Ask to pick yourself:**
- **You:** Mogę sam/sama wybrać? `(MO-geh sam/SA-ma VIH-brach)` - Can I pick myself?
- **Vendor:** *Might say yes or look annoyed*

**Option B - Point to better ones:**
- **You:** Tamte, poproszę. `(TAM-te, po-PRO-sheh)` - Those ones, please. *point*
- **You:** Te są lepsze. `(te soh LEP-she)` - These are better. *point*

**Option C - Reject the bad ones:**
- **You:** Nie te. Inne. `(nyeh te. EEN-ne)` - Not those. Different ones.

**Option D - Just accept it:**
- *Sometimes not worth the fight*
- Check fruit before paying if you can

---

## Complication 2: Price Confusion (Per Kilo vs Per Piece)

**You thought it was 5 zł per kilo, but it's 5 zł per piece.**

**Vendor:** Pięć złotych za sztukę. `(pyench ZWO-tih za SHTOO-keh)` - Five złoty per piece.

**You thought it was per kilo:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, to za kilogram? `(psheh-PRA-sham, to za kee-lo-GRAM)` - Sorry, is that per kilo?
- **Vendor:** Nie, za sztukę. `(nyeh, za SHTOO-keh)` - No, per piece.

**If too expensive:**
- **You:** To za drogo. `(to za DRO-go)` - That's too expensive.
- **You:** *Put it back and walk away*

**Price per unit vocabulary:**
- Za kilogram `(za kee-lo-GRAM)` - Per kilogram
- Za sztukę `(za SHTOO-keh)` - Per piece
- Za łodyżkę `(za wo-DIHZH-keh)` - Per bunch (for herbs)

---

## Complication 3: Vendor Speaks Fast or Mumbles

**The vendor says something but you don't understand.**

**Vendor:** *rapid Polish*

**Your options:**

**Option A - Ask to repeat:**
- **You:** Przepraszam? `(psheh-PRA-sham)` - Sorry?
- **You:** Wolniej proszę. `(VOL-nyey PRO-sheh)` - Slower please.

**Option B - Ask specific question:**
- **You:** Ile? `(EE-leh)` - How much? *point at what you want*
- **You:** Ile kosztuje? `(EE-leh kosh-TOO-yeh)` - How much does it cost?

**Option C - Move to different stall:**
- *Sometimes easier to find a different vendor*

---

## Complication 4: They Add Extras You Didn't Ask For

**You asked for apples, but they're adding tomatoes to your bag too.**

**Vendor:** Może jeszcze pomidory? `(MO-zheh YESH-cheh po-mee-DO-rih)` - Maybe also tomatoes?
*They start putting them in your bag*

**Your options:**

**Option A - Stop them:**
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. Tylko jabłka. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh. TIL-ko YAB-wka)` - No thanks. Only apples.
- **You:** *Wave hand* Stop. Nie. `(nyeh)` - No.

**Option B - If already in bag:**
- **You:** Proszę wyjąć. `(PRO-sheh VIH-yonch)` - Please take out.
- **You:** Nie chcę tego. `(nyeh htse TE-go)` - I don't want this.

---

## Complication 5: They Put Too Much/Too Little

**You asked for 1 kilo but they put 1.5.**

**Vendor:** Półtora kilo, dobrze? `(poow-TO-ra KEE-lo, DOB-zheh)` - One and a half kilos, okay?

**If too much:**
- **You:** Mniej. Tylko kilo. `(mnyey. TIL-ko KEE-lo)` - Less. Only one kilo.

**If too little:**
- **You:** Więcej, poproszę. `(VYEN-tsey, po-PRO-sheh)` - More, please.

**"This much" conversation:**
- **Vendor:** Tyle? `(TIH-leh)` - This much? *holds up bag*
- **You:** Tak. `(tak)` - Yes.
- **You:** Więcej. `(VYEN-tsey)` - More.
- **You:** Mniej. `(mnyey)` - Less.

---

## Complication 6: Cash Only - You Don't Have Enough

**Vendor:** Tylko gotówka. `(TIL-ko go-TOOF-ka)` - Cash only.

**If you don't have enough cash:**
- **You:** Nie mam gotówki. `(nyeh mam go-TOOF-kee)` - I don't have cash.
- **You:** Gdzie jest bankomat? `(gjeh yest ban-KO-mat)` - Where is an ATM?

**If you only have a big bill:**
- **You:** *Show 100 zł bill* Mogę tak? `(MO-geh tak)` - Can I [pay with] this?
- **Vendor:** *Might say they don't have change*

---

## Complication 7: You Changed Your Mind

**You started ordering but want to stop.**

**You:** Przepraszam, nie chcę. Dziękuję. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh htse. jen-KOO-yeh)` - Sorry, I don't want [it]. Thank you.

**If they already weighed/bagged it:**
- This is awkward but you can still walk away
- Say "Przepraszam" and leave

## Cultural Notes

- **Cash only**: Most market vendors don't take cards. Bring cash!
- **Vendors choose for you**: They usually pick the fruit. Asking to choose yourself is okay but not always welcome.
- **Bargaining**: Possible at end of day, but not expected. "To za drogo" might get a small discount.
- **Bring your own bag**: Most vendors don't provide bags, or charge for them.
- **Seasonal**: Selection varies. Summer has most variety.

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- "Dzień dobry"
- "Poproszę jabłka, kilo" *hold up one finger*
- Pay cash, say "Dziękuję"

### Challenge Version
- Specify exactly: "Pół kilo truskawek, poproszę"
- If they pick bad ones: "Tamte, poproszę" *point to better ones*
- Check price: "Ile za kilogram?"
