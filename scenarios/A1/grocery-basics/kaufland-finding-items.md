## Scenario: Finding Items at Kaufland

### Your Goal
You're looking for coffee (kawa) but can't find it. You need to ask a store employee where it is.

### Setting
You're walking through Kaufland and can't find the coffee aisle. You see an employee stocking shelves.

---

## Dialogue Variations

### Getting the Employee's Attention

**You:** Przepraszam. `(psheh-PRA-sham)` - Excuse me.

**Employee responses:**
- **Response A:** Tak? `(tak)` - Yes?
- **Response B:** Słucham? `(SWOO-ham)` - Yes? / I'm listening?
- **Response C:** Proszę? `(PRO-sheh)` - Yes?
- **Response D:** Czym mogę pomóc? `(chim MO-geh PO-moots)` - How can I help?

---

### Asking Where Something Is

**You:** Gdzie jest kawa? `(gjeh yest KA-va)` - Where is coffee?

**Alternative phrasings:**
- **You:** Szukam kawy. `(SHOO-kam KA-vih)` - I'm looking for coffee.
- **You:** Gdzie mogę znaleźć kawę? `(gjeh MO-geh zna-LESHCH KA-veh)` - Where can I find coffee?

---

### Employee's Responses

**Variation A - Simple directions:**
- **Employee:** Tam, na lewo. `(tam, na LE-vo)` - There, on the left.
- **You:** Dziękuję. `(jen-KOO-yeh)` - Thank you.

**Variation B - More detailed:**
- **Employee:** Trzeci rząd, na prawo. `(TSHEH-chee zhond, na PRA-vo)` - Third aisle, on the right.
- **You:** Dziękuję bardzo. `(jen-KOO-yeh BAR-dzo)` - Thank you very much.

**Variation C - They show you:**
- **Employee:** Proszę za mną. `(PRO-sheh za mnog)` - Follow me, please.
- **Employee:** *walks you to the coffee aisle*
- **Employee:** Tutaj. `(TOO-tay)` - Here.
- **You:** Dziękuję bardzo! `(jen-KOO-yeh BAR-dzo)` - Thank you very much!

**Variation D - They point:**
- **Employee:** *Points* Tam. `(tam)` - There.
- **You:** Dziękuję. `(jen-KOO-yeh)` - Thank you.

**Variation E - It's right behind you:**
- **Employee:** Za panem/panią. `(za PA-nem/PA-nyong)` - Behind you. *(pa-nem for men, pa-nyą for women)*
- **You:** Aha! Dziękuję. `(a-HA! jen-KOO-yeh)` - Ah! Thank you.

## Pronunciation Tips

1. **"Gdzie"**: Sounds like "gjeh" - the "dz" is soft
2. **"Szukam"**: SHOO-kam, the "sz" is like "sh"
3. **"Lewo" vs "Prawo"**: LE-vo (left), PRA-vo (right). Don't confuse them!
4. **"Rząd"**: Difficult sound - "zhond" with a nasal "ą"
5. **"Trzeci"**: TSHEH-chee, the "trz" is challenging - practice it!

---

## Cultural Notes

- **Employees are generally helpful**: Most store employees will help you, even if they're busy. Don't hesitate to ask!
- **Pointing is okay**: If you can't pronounce something, pointing or showing a picture on your phone is perfectly acceptable.
- **Learn the layout**: After shopping a few times, you'll know where things are. But asking is always okay!
- **Aisle signs**: Many stores have signs at the end of aisles listing what's in that aisle - look up!

## Common Complications

**What if you don't understand their directions?**

- **You:** Przepraszam, nie rozumiem. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh ro-ZOO-myem)` - Sorry, I don't understand.
- **Employee:** *Usually points or shows you directly*

**What if they don't know?**

- **Employee:** Nie wiem. `(nyeh vyem)` - I don't know.
- **Employee:** Proszę zapytać tam. `(PRO-sheh za-PIT-tach tam)` - Please ask over there.
- **You:** Dobrze, dziękuję. `(DOB-zheh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - Okay, thank you.

**What if you forget the Polish word?**

- **You:** Przepraszam, szukam... *show your phone with image/word*
- Most employees will understand and help!

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- "Przepraszam"
- "Gdzie jest kawa?" *(or point to your shopping list)*
- "Dziękuję"

### Challenge Version
- Use "Szukam..." (I'm looking for...)
- Try asking about multiple items
- Follow up with: "Czy jest jeszcze gdzie indziej?" (Is it anywhere else?) if you can't find it
