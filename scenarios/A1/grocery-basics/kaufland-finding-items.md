## Scenario: Finding Items When Directions Confuse You

### Your Goal
You can't find something in the store. You'll ask an employee, but they might: not understand what you're looking for, give confusing directions you can't follow, or just not know. Learn to handle all of it.

### Setting
You're wandering around Kaufland looking for coffee (or milk, or bread...). You've been walking in circles. Time to ask for help - but this might not go smoothly.

---

## The Basic Question

**You:** Przepraszam, gdzie jest kawa? `(psheh-PRA-sham, gjeh yest KA-va)` - Excuse me, where is coffee?

**Alternative:**
- Szukam kawy. `(SHOO-kam KA-vih)` - I'm looking for coffee.
- Gdzie mogę znaleźć kawę? `(gjeh MO-geh zna-LESHCH KA-veh)` - Where can I find coffee?

---

## Complication 1: They Don't Understand What You Want

**Your pronunciation of "kawa" sounds like... something else.**

**Employee:** Kawa? `(KA-va)` - Coffee? *confused look*
**Employee:** Co pan szuka? `(tso pan SHOO-ka)` - What are you looking for?
**Employee:** Nie rozumiem. `(nyeh ro-ZOO-myem)` - I don't understand.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Describe it:**
- **You:** Kawa... do picia. Czarna. `(KA-va... do PEE-cha. CHAR-na)` - Coffee... for drinking. Black.
- *Make drinking motion*

**Option B - Show a picture:**
- **You:** *Show phone with image of coffee*
- **Employee:** Aaaa, kawa! `(ah-ah, KA-va)` - Ah, coffee!

**Option C - Spell it out:**
- **You:** K-A-W-A. `(ka, ah, vu, ah)` - *spell Polish alphabet style*
- *This sometimes helps*

**Option D - Point at someone's cart:**
- **You:** *See someone with coffee in their cart* - Tam! To! `(tam! to!)` - There! That!
- **Employee:** *Now they get it*

---

## Complication 2: They Give Directions You Can't Follow

**They say a bunch of Polish you don't understand.**

**Employee:** Proszę iść prosto, potem na lewo, trzeci rząd, półka na górze, obok kaszy. `(long confusing directions)`

**Your brain:** ???

**Your options:**

**Option A - Admit you're lost:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, nie rozumiem. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh ro-ZOO-myem)` - Sorry, I don't understand.

**Option B - Ask for simpler directions:**
- **You:** Lewo czy prawo? `(LE-vo chih PRA-vo)` - Left or right?
- **You:** Który rząd? `(KTOO-rih zhond)` - Which aisle?

**Option C - Ask them to show you:**
- **You:** Proszę pokazać? `(PRO-sheh po-KA-zach)` - Please show [me]?
- **You:** Może pan/pani pokazać? `(MO-zheh pan/PA-nee po-KA-zach)` - Can you show [me]?

**Option D - Repeat what you understood:**
- **You:** Na lewo? `(na LE-vo)` - On the left?
- **Employee:** *Nods or corrects*

**What if they point vaguely?:**
- **Employee:** Tam. `(tam)` - There. *waves hand in general direction*
- **You:** Tam? `(tam)` - There? *point in same direction*
- **Employee:** Tak. `(tak)` - Yes. *or corrects your pointing*

## Complication 3: The Item Doesn't Exist Here

**They don't have what you want.**

**Employee:** Nie mamy. `(nyeh MA-mih)` - We don't have [it].
**Employee:** Skończyło się. `(skon-CHI-wo sheh)` - It's sold out.
**Employee:** Nie prowadzimy. `(nyeh pro-va-JEE-mih)` - We don't carry [it].

**Your options:**

**Option A - Ask when:**
- **You:** Kiedy będzie? `(KYEH-dih BEN-jeh)` - When will it be [available]?

**Option B - Ask where else:**
- **You:** Gdzie mogę kupić? `(gjeh MO-geh KOO-peech)` - Where can I buy [it]?

**Option C - Accept it:**
- **You:** Rozumiem. Dziękuję. `(ro-ZOO-myem. jen-KOO-yeh)` - I understand. Thank you.

---

## Complication 4: They Point But You Still Can't Find It

**They gave directions. You walked there. It's not there.**

**What to do:**

**Option A - Go back and ask again:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, nie mogę znaleźć. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh MO-geh zna-LESHCH)` - Sorry, I can't find [it].

**Option B - Ask someone else:**
- Different employees know different things
- **You:** Przepraszam, szukam kawy. `(psheh-PRA-sham, SHOO-kam KA-vih)`

**Option C - Look for aisle signs:**
- Look up at the signs above aisles
- Common words: KAWA (coffee), HERBATA (tea), NAPOJE (drinks), NABIAŁ (dairy)

**Option D - Just ask another customer:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, gdzie jest kawa? `(psheh-PRA-sham, gjeh yest KA-va)`
- *Regular shoppers often know the store better*

---

## Complication 5: They Don't Know

**Employee:** Nie wiem. `(nyeh vyem)` - I don't know.
**Employee:** Nie jestem stąd. `(nyeh YES-tem stond)` - I'm not from here. *new employee*

**Your options:**

**Option A - Ask where to ask:**
- **You:** Kto może wiedzieć? `(kto MO-zheh VYEH-jech)` - Who might know?

**Option B - Ask at the register:**
- **You:** Przy kasie zapytam? `(pshih KA-sheh za-PIH-tam)` - Should I ask at the register?

**Option C - Thank them and try someone else:**
- **You:** Dobrze, dziękuję. `(DOB-zheh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - Okay, thank you.

---

## Complication 6: You Asked for the Wrong Thing

**You said the wrong word and they took you to the wrong place.**

**You thought you asked for milk (mleko) but ended up at flour (mąka).**

**Your options:**

**Option A - Try again:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, nie to. Mleko. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh to. MLE-ko)` - Sorry, not that. Milk.

**Option B - Show what you mean:**
- **You:** *Make drinking motion* Mleko? Do picia? `(MLE-ko? do PEE-cha)` - Milk? For drinking?

**Option C - Use your phone:**
- **You:** *Show picture of what you actually want*

## Cultural Notes

- **Pointing is normal**: If you can't understand, pointing and gesturing is totally fine.
- **Employees may be busy**: Don't take brusque answers personally.
- **Store layouts vary**: Even the same chain can be arranged differently in different locations.
- **"Tam" with vague gesture**: Very common. They're not being unhelpful - they just expect you to walk that direction and look.

---

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- Say "Przepraszam" to get attention
- Say "Gdzie jest [item]?" or just "[item]?" with questioning tone
- If confused, say "Proszę pokazać?" and gesture "show me"

### Challenge Version
- Ask full question: "Przepraszam, gdzie mogę znaleźć kawę?"
- Confirm directions: "Na lewo, tak?"
- If it's not there: "Nie mogę znaleźć"
