## Scenario: Surviving Checkout Disasters

### Your Goal
Handle the chaos when things go wrong at checkout: card declined, terminal errors, security tags beeping, needing to call a supervisor. These situations are stressful even in your native language.

### Setting
You're at checkout in a Polish supermarket. Something has gone wrong and you need to deal with it using limited Polish.

---

## Problem 1: Card Declined / Terminal Error

**The card machine isn't working or your card is rejected.**

**Cashier:** Karta nie działa. `(KAR-ta nyeh JAH-wa)` - Card isn't working.
**Cashier:** Proszę jeszcze raz. `(PRO-sheh YESH-cheh raz)` - Please [try] again.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Try again:**
- **You:** Jeszcze raz? `(YESH-cheh raz)` - Again?
- *Try card again*

**Option B - Try different card:**
- **You:** Inna karta. `(EEN-na KAR-ta)` - Different card.
- *Pull out another card*

**Option C - Use cash:**
- **You:** Gotówką. `(go-TOOF-koh)` - With cash.
- *This is often the easiest solution*

**Option D - Ask what's wrong:**
- **You:** Co jest nie tak? `(tso yest nyeh tak)` - What's wrong?
- *They might explain - or you won't understand anyway*

---

## Problem 2: PIN Error

**Cashier:** Zły PIN. `(zwih peen)` - Wrong PIN.
**Cashier:** Proszę PIN. `(PRO-sheh peen)` - Please [enter] PIN.

**Your response:**
- **You:** Przepraszam. Jeszcze raz. `(psheh-PRA-sham. YESH-cheh raz)` - Sorry. Again.
- *Enter PIN carefully*

**If PIN fails multiple times:**
- **You:** Gotówką. `(go-TOOF-koh)` - With cash.
- *Switch to cash before card gets blocked*

---

## Problem 3: Self-Checkout Machine Yelling

**The machine speaks only Polish. Something went wrong.**

**Machine:** Nieoczekiwany przedmiot! `(nyeh-o-che-KEE-va-nih psheh-JMYOT)` - Unexpected item!
**Machine:** Proszę czekać na obsługę. `(PRO-sheh CHE-kach na op-SWOO-geh)` - Please wait for assistance.

**What to do:**

**Option A - Wait for attendant:**
- Stand there looking helpless
- Say "Przepraszam" when they come
- Point at screen

**Option B - Call for help:**
- **You:** Przepraszam! `(psheh-PRA-sham)` - Excuse me!
- *Wave at attendant*

**Option C - Just remove the item:**
- Take item off bagging area
- Scan again slowly

---

## Problem 4: Age Verification (Alcohol)

**You're buying alcohol. Machine or cashier needs to verify age.**

**Machine:** Proszę czekać na weryfikację wieku. `(PRO-sheh CHE-kach na ve-ree-fee-KA-tsyeh VYEH-koo)` - Please wait for age verification.

**Cashier:** Poproszę dowód. `(po-PRO-sheh do-VOOT)` - ID please.
**Cashier:** Dokument? `(do-koo-MENT)` - Document?

**Your response:**
- **You:** Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)` - Here you go. *show ID*
- **You:** Paszport? `(PASH-port)` - Passport? *if unsure what they want*

---

## Problem 5: Security Tag Beeping

**You've paid and are leaving. Security alarm goes off.**

**Guard:** Przepraszam! `(psheh-PRA-sham)` - Excuse me!
**Guard:** Paragon? `(pa-RA-gon)` - Receipt?

**Your response:**

- **You:** Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)` - Here. *show receipt*
- **You:** Zapłaciłem/am. `(za-pwa-CHEE-wem/am)` - I paid. *(m/f)*

**If they want to check your bag:**
- **You:** Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)` - Here/Please. *open bag*
- Stay calm, this happens to everyone sometimes

**If tag wasn't removed:**
- **You:** *Point to item* Klipsa? `(KLIP-sa)` - Tag?
- They'll remove it

---

## Problem 6: Item Won't Scan

**The barcode doesn't scan. Cashier is frustrated.**

**Cashier:** Nie skanuje. `(nyeh ska-NOO-yeh)` - It won't scan.
**Cashier:** *Typing manually or calling someone*

**What you might hear:**

**Cashier:** Ile to kosztuje? `(EE-leh to kosh-TOO-yeh)` - How much is this?
- **You:** Nie wiem. `(nyeh vyem)` - I don't know.
- **You:** *Show price with fingers or phone*

**Cashier:** Proszę czekać. `(PRO-sheh CHE-kach)` - Please wait.
- **You:** Dobrze. `(DOB-zheh)` - Okay.

**If they ask you to get another one:**
- **Cashier:** *Points to shelves* Może inny? `(MO-zheh EEN-nih)` - Maybe another one?
- **You:** Dobrze. `(DOB-zheh)` - Okay. *go get another*
- Or: Nie chcę. `(nyeh htse)` - I don't want [it]. *skip item*

---

## Problem 7: Cashier Calls Supervisor

**Something is wrong. Cashier picks up phone or waves to someone.**

**Cashier:** Proszę chwilę poczekać. `(PRO-sheh HVEE-leh po-CHE-kach)` - Please wait a moment.
**Cashier:** Kierownik! `(kyeh-ROV-neek)` - Supervisor!

**Your options:**

**Just wait:**
- This might take a while
- Say "Dobrze" (okay) and wait

**If you're in a hurry:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, nie chcę tego. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh htse TE-go)` - Sorry, I don't want this. *point to problem item*
- Let them remove it and continue

---

## Problem 8: You Don't Have Enough Money

**You realize you can't afford everything.**

**Cashier:** [Total amount] złotych.
**You:** *Check wallet - not enough*

**Your options:**

**Option A - Remove something:**
- **You:** Przepraszam. Proszę wyjąć to. `(psheh-PRA-sham. PRO-sheh VIH-yonch to)` - Sorry. Please take this out. *point*

**Option B - Apologize and leave:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, nie mam tyle. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh mam TIH-leh)` - Sorry, I don't have that much.
- This is embarrassing but it happens

## Cultural Notes

- **Carry cash backup**: Card failures happen. Having cash saves embarrassment.
- **Self-checkout**: Machines speak only Polish. Attendants are usually nearby and used to helping confused foreigners.
- **Security alarms**: Don't panic. Show your receipt and it's fine. Tags get left on sometimes.
- **Patience**: Polish service can be slower than you're used to. Saying "Proszę czekać" is normal.
- **Supervisor calls**: These happen for many reasons (price checks, errors). It's not about you personally.

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- Card fails: "Gotówką" (pay cash)
- Wait: "Dobrze"
- Security: Show receipt, say "Proszę"

### Challenge Version
- Ask what's wrong: "Co jest nie tak?"
- Request item removal: "Proszę wyjąć to"
- Handle age verification with ID
