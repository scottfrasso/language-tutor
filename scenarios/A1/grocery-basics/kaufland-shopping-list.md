## Scenario: Shopping with a List at Kaufland

### Your Goal
You have a small shopping list: milk (mleko), bread (chleb), and apples (jabłka). You need to find these items and check out successfully.

### Setting
You're at Kaufland supermarket with your three items. You've gathered everything and are at the checkout line.

---

## At the Checkout

### Opening

**Cashier:** Dzień dobry! `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day!

**You:** Dzień dobry. `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day.

---

### Scanning Items

**The cashier scans your three items: milk, bread, apples**

**Variation A - Standard (they tell you total):**
- **Cashier:** Dwadzieścia trzy złote. `(dva-JESH-cha tshih ZWO-teh)` - Twenty-three złoty.
- **You:** *Hand over money*

**Variation B - They ask about bags:**
- **Cashier:** Reklamówka? `(re-kla-MOOF-ka)` - [Plastic] bag?
  OR: Torba? `(TOR-ba)` - Bag?
- **You:** Tak, poproszę. `(tak, po-PRO-sheh)` - Yes, please.
  OR: Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you. *(if you have your own)*

**Variation C - They ask about card:**
- **Cashier:** Kartą? `(KAR-tong)` - By card?
- **You:** Tak. `(tak)` - Yes.
  OR: Nie, gotówką. `(nyeh, go-TOOF-kong)` - No, cash.

---

### If Asked About Receipt

**Cashier:** Paragon? `(pa-RA-gon)` - Receipt?

**You:**
- **Yes:** Tak, poproszę. `(tak, po-PRO-sheh)` - Yes, please.
- **No:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.

---

### Payment

**You:** Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)` - Here you go. *hands over card or money*

**If using card:**
- **Cashier:** *gestures to terminal* Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)`
- **You:** *Tap or insert card*

**If receiving change:**
- **Cashier:** I dwa złote reszty. `(ee dva ZWO-teh RESH-tih)` - And two złoty change.
- **You:** Dziękuję. `(jen-KOO-yeh)` - Thank you.

---

### Closing

**Cashier:** Do widzenia! `(do vee-JEN-ya)` - Goodbye!

**You:** Dziękuję, do widzenia! `(jen-KOO-yeh, do vee-JEN-ya)` - Thank you, goodbye!

## Pronunciation Tips

1. **"Mleko"**: Don't skip the "l" - MLE-ko, not "meko"
2. **"Jabłka"**: The "ł" sounds like "w" - YAB-wka
3. **"Reklamówka"**: re-kla-MOOF-ka, stress on MOOF
4. **"Paragon"**: Sounds like English "paragon" but with Polish "r" (rolled slightly)
5. **"Kartą" vs "Gotówką"**: Both end in nasal "ą" sound (like "ong")

---

## Cultural Notes

- **Plastic bags cost money**: In Poland, plastic bags (reklamówki) at stores cost around 0,20-0,50 zł each. Most people bring their own bags or buy reusable ones.
- **Receipts**: Cashiers always ask if you want the receipt (paragon). You can say no if you don't need it.
- **Packing your own groceries**: In most Polish supermarkets, you pack your own bags. Be quick! There might be people waiting behind you.
- **Cash vs Card**: Both are widely accepted. Card payments are very common, even for small amounts.

## Common Complications

**What if they ask about a loyalty card?**

- **Cashier:** Karta Payback? `(KAR-ta PAYBACK)` - Payback card? *(Kaufland loyalty card)*
- **You:** Nie mam. `(nyeh mam)` - I don't have [one].
  OR: Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.

**What if you forgot something?**

- **You:** Przepraszam, zapomniałem/zapomniałam. `(psheh-PRA-sham, za-pom-NYA-wem/wam)` - Sorry, I forgot.
  *(Use -łem if you're male, -łam if you're female)*
- Then step out of line and come back later

**What if the total is too much?**

- **You:** Ile? `(EE-leh)` - How much?
- **Cashier:** *repeats the amount*
- Look at the register display to see the number

## Practice Variations

### Easiest Version
- Say "Dzień dobry"
- Put items on counter
- When asked about bags: "Tak" or "Nie"
- Pay when they tell you the amount
- Say "Dziękuję, do widzenia"

### Challenge Version
- Bring your own bag and say: "Mam torbę" (I have a bag)
- Ask for the receipt: "Poproszę paragon"
- Count your change to make sure it's correct
- If they ask about loyalty card, politely decline: "Nie, dziękuję"
