## Scenario: Custom Cake Orders That Don't Go Smoothly

### Your Goal
You want to order a custom cake, but complications arise: they misunderstand the inscription, your flavor isn't available, the price is higher than expected, or the cake looks wrong at pickup.

### Setting
You're at a cukiernia (pastry shop) ordering a custom cake. Things might not go as planned.

---

## Complication 1: They Misunderstand the Inscription

**You want "Sto lat Ania" but they write something else.**

**You:** Poproszę napis "Sto lat Ania." `(po-PRO-sheh NA-pees sto lat A-nya)` - I'd like the inscription "Happy Birthday Ania."

**Attendant:** Ania z Y czy z I? `(A-nya z ih-GREK chih z ee)` - Ania with Y or I?
**You:** Z I. A-N-I-A. `(z ee. a-en-ee-a)` - With I. A-N-I-A.

**At pickup - wrong spelling:**

**You:** Przepraszam, to jest źle napisane. To Ania, nie Anna. `(psheh-PRA-sham, to yest zhleh na-PEE-sa-neh. to A-nya, nyeh AN-na)` - Sorry, this is spelled wrong. It's Ania, not Anna.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Ask them to fix it:**
- **You:** Czy mogą państwo poprawić? `(chih MO-goh PAN-stvo po-PRA-veech)` - Can you fix it?

**Option B - Accept if no time:**
- **You:** Kiedy może być gotowe? `(KYEH-dih MO-zheh bich go-TO-veh)` - When can it be ready?
- *Decide if you can wait*

**Option C - Ask for discount:**
- **You:** To nie moja wina. Może zniżka? `(to nyeh MO-ya VEE-na. MO-zheh ZNEEZH-ka)` - This isn't my fault. Maybe a discount?

---

## Complication 2: Flavor Not Available

**They don't have your chosen flavor.**

**You:** Poproszę tort czekoladowy. `(po-PRO-sheh tort che-ko-la-DO-vih)` - I'd like a chocolate cake.

**Attendant:** Niestety nie mamy czekolady na ten termin. `(nyes-TE-tih nyeh MA-mih che-ko-LA-dih na ten TER-meen)` - Unfortunately we don't have chocolate for that date.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Ask what's available:**
- **You:** Jakie smaki są dostępne? `(YA-kyeh SMA-kee song do-STEMP-neh)` - What flavors are available?

**Option B - Choose alternative:**
- **You:** A waniliowy? `(a va-nee-LYO-vih)` - And vanilla?
- **Attendant:** Tak, mamy. `(tak, MA-mih)` - Yes, we have.

**Option C - Change date:**
- **You:** Kiedy będzie czekolada? `(KYEH-dih BEN-jeh che-ko-LA-da)` - When will chocolate be available?

---

## Complication 3: Price Higher Than Expected

**The final price is more than they originally said.**

**Original quote:** Osiemdziesiąt złotych. `(o-shem-JEH-shont ZWO-tih)` - Eighty złoty.

**At pickup:**

**Attendant:** Sto dwadzieścia złotych. `(sto dva-JESH-cha ZWO-tih)` - One hundred twenty złoty.

**You:** Ale pani mówiła osiemdziesiąt! `(ALE PA-nee MOO-vee-wa o-shem-JEH-shont)` - But you said eighty!

**Attendant:** Napis dodatkowy kosztuje. I większy rozmiar. `(NA-pees do-dat-KO-vih kosh-TOO-ye. ee VYENK-shih ROZ-myar)` - The inscription costs extra. And larger size.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Argue:**
- **You:** Nikt mi tego nie powiedział. `(neekt mee TE-go nyeh po-VYEH-jaw)` - Nobody told me that.

**Option B - Pay but express displeasure:**
- **You:** Dobrze, ale następnym razem proszę powiedzieć wcześniej. `(DOB-zheh, ALE nas-TEMP-nim RA-zem PRO-sheh po-VYEH-jech VCHESH-nyey)` - Okay, but next time please tell me earlier.

**Option C - Negotiate:**
- **You:** Może dziewięćdziesiąt? Nie wiedziałem. `(MO-zheh jeh-vyench-jeh-SHONT? nyeh vyeh-JA-wem)` - Maybe ninety? I didn't know.

---

## Complication 4: Cake Looks Different Than Expected

**The cake doesn't look like what you imagined.**

**You:** *See cake* To nie wygląda jak na zdjęciu. `(to nyeh vih-GLON-da yak na ZDYEN-choo)` - This doesn't look like in the picture.

**Attendant:** To jest ręcznie robione. Zawsze trochę inaczej. `(to yest RENCH-nyeh ro-BYO-neh. ZAV-sheh TRO-heh ee-NA-chey)` - This is handmade. Always a bit different.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Accept it:**
- **You:** Dobrze. `(DOB-zheh)` - Okay.
- *Maybe it's fine*

**Option B - Ask for changes:**
- **You:** Czy można dodać więcej kwiatków? `(chih MOZH-na do-DACH VYEN-tsey kvyat-KOOF)` - Can you add more flowers?

**Option C - Refuse:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, ale to nie jest to, co zamówiłem. `(psheh-PRA-sham, ALE to nyeh yest to, tso za-MOO-vee-wem)` - Sorry, but this isn't what I ordered.

---

## Complication 5: They Can't Do Your Timeline

**You need it tomorrow. They can't make it.**

**You:** Na jutro, poproszę. `(na YOO-tro, po-PRO-sheh)` - For tomorrow, please.

**Attendant:** Na jutro nie możemy. Minimum trzy dni. `(na YOO-tro nyeh MO-zheh-mih. MEE-nee-moom tshih dnee)` - We can't do tomorrow. Minimum three days.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Ask about rush order:**
- **You:** Może za dodatkową opłatę? `(MO-zheh za do-dat-KO-voh op-WA-teh)` - Maybe for an extra fee?

**Option B - Change order:**
- **You:** A gotowy tort? Bez napisu? `(a go-TO-vih tort? bez NA-pee-soo)` - And a ready-made cake? Without inscription?

**Option C - Try another shop:**
- **You:** Rozumiem. Dziękuję. `(ro-ZOO-myem. jen-KOO-yeh)` - I understand. Thank you.
- *Go somewhere else*

---

## Complication 6: They Forgot Your Order

**You arrive and they have no record of your order.**

**You:** Dzień dobry. Przyszedłem/am po tort. Zamówiłem/am na piątek. `(jen DOB-rih. pshih-SHED-wem/am po tort. za-MOO-vee-wem/am na PYON-tek)` - Good day. I came for a cake. I ordered for Friday.

**Attendant:** Jakie nazwisko? `(YA-kyeh naz-VEES-ko)` - What name?
**You:** [Surname]
**Attendant:** Przepraszam, nie mamy takiego zamówienia. `(psheh-PRA-sham, nyeh MA-mih ta-KYEH-go za-MOO-vyeh-nya)` - Sorry, we don't have that order.

**Your options:**

**Option A - Show proof:**
- **You:** Mam paragon. Zapłaciłem zaliczkę. `(mam pa-RA-gon. za-pwa-CHEE-wem za-LEECH-keh)` - I have a receipt. I paid a deposit.

**Option B - Describe what you ordered:**
- **You:** Tort czekoladowy z napisem "Sto lat Ania." `(tort che-ko-la-DO-vih z NA-pee-sem sto lat A-nya)` - Chocolate cake with inscription "Happy Birthday Ania."

**Option C - Ask for emergency solution:**
- **You:** Potrzebuję tort dzisiaj. Co mogą państwo zrobić? `(po-TSHEH-boo-yeh tort JEE-shay. tso MO-goh PAN-stvo ZRO-beech)` - I need a cake today. What can you do?

## Cultural Notes

- **Deposits are common**: Many shops require 50% deposit for custom orders. Keep your receipt!
- **Handmade variance**: Polish bakers often emphasize "ręcznie robione" (handmade) - expect some variation from pictures.
- **Advance notice**: Most custom cakes need 2-3 days minimum. Rush orders cost extra.
- **Inscriptions in Polish**: "Sto lat" is the standard birthday phrase. Spelling foreign names can cause confusion.
- **Price surprises**: Always confirm final price including all extras (inscription, size, decoration).

## Practice Variations

### Intermediate Version
- Order cake: "Tort czekoladowy na sobotę"
- If problem: "Przepraszam, to jest źle"
- Accept or negotiate

### Advanced Version
- Detailed order: "Tort dla dziesięciu osób, waniliowy, z napisem 'Sto lat Marek', odbiorę w piątek o czternastej"
- Handle problem: "To nie wygląda jak na zdjęciu. Czy można poprawić?"
- Negotiate: "Pani mówiła osiemdziesiąt, nie sto dwadzieścia"
