## Scenario: Shopping at Multiple Stalls

### Your Goal
You want to buy tomatoes and apples, but from different vendors. You need to compare prices and quality.

### Setting
You're at the farmers market. You see multiple vendors selling similar products. You want to find the best quality and price.

---

## At First Vendor (Tomatoes)

### Asking About Product

**You:** Dzień dobry. Ile kosztują pomidory? `(jen DOB-rih. EE-leh kosh-TOO-yong po-mee-DO-rih)` - Good day. How much are the tomatoes?

**Vendor 1:** Dziesięć złotych za kilogram. `(JEH-shench ZWO-tih za kee-lo-GRAM)` - Ten złoty per kilogram.

**You:** Czy są ekologiczne? `(chih song e-ko-lo-GEECH-neh)` - Are they organic?

**Vendor 1:** Tak, bez chemii. Z mojego ogrodu. `(tak, bez HEH-mee-ee. z mo-YEH-go o-GRO-doo)` - Yes, without chemicals. From my garden.

**You:** Dziękuję, pomyślę. `(jen-KOO-yeh, po-MISH-leh)` - Thank you, I'll think about it.

---

## At Second Vendor (Comparing)

**You:** Dzień dobry. A u pana ile kosztują pomidory? `(jen DOB-rih. a oo PA-na EE-leh kosh-TOO-yong po-mee-DO-rih)` - Good day. And how much are your tomatoes?

**Vendor 2:** Osiem złotych. `(O-shem ZWO-tih)` - Eight złoty.

**You:** Są świeże? `(song SHVYEH-zheh)` - Are they fresh?

**Vendor 2:** Z dzisiaj rano. Proszę spróbować. `(z JEE-shay RA-no. PRO-sheh SPROO-bo-vach)` - From this morning. Please try one.

**You:** *taste* Dobre! Wezmę dwa kilo. `(DOB-reh! VEZ-meh dva KEE-lo)` - Good! I'll take two kilos.

---

## At Third Vendor (Apples)

**You:** Dzień dobry. Szukam jabłek. Które są najlepsze? `(jen DOB-rih. SHOO-kam YAB-wek. KTOO-reh song nay-LEP-sheh)` - Good day. I'm looking for apples. Which are the best?

**Vendor 3:** Te są słodkie, a te są kwaśne. `(teh song SWOT-kyeh, a teh song KVASH-neh)` - These are sweet, and these are sour.

**You:** Wolę słodkie. `(VO-leh SWOT-kyeh)` - I prefer sweet.

**Vendor 3:** To polecam te. Bardzo dobre. `(to po-LEH-tsam teh. BAR-dzo DOB-reh)` - Then I recommend these. Very good.

**You:** Ile? `(EE-leh)` - How much?

**Vendor 3:** Dwanaście za kilo. `(dva-NASH-cheh za KEE-lo)` - Twelve per kilo.

**You:** Wezmę półtora kilo. `(VEZ-meh poow-TO-ra KEE-lo)` - I'll take one and a half kilos.

## Cultural Notes

- **Shopping around is normal**: It's completely acceptable to ask prices at multiple stalls before buying.
- **"Pomyślę"**: Saying "I'll think about it" is a polite way to move on without committing to buy.
- **Tasting is encouraged**: Many vendors offer samples (próbki). It's normal to taste before buying.
- **"Z ogrodu" / "Bez chemii"**: Vendors selling their own produce often advertise it - generally means good quality and truly fresh.
- **Compare openly**: You can openly ask "A u pana?" (And at your place?) to compare prices at different stalls.

---

## Practice Variations

### Intermediate Version
- Ask prices at two stalls
- Choose cheaper one
- "Wezmę kilo"

### Advanced Version
- Compare quality: "Które są świeższe?" (Which are fresher?)
- Negotiate: "U niego są taniej" (At his place they're cheaper)
- Explain preference: "Wolę ekologiczne" (I prefer organic)
