## Scenario: Surviving Aggressive Upsells

### Your Goal
At checkout, you'll face a barrage of offers: loyalty cards, apps, charity donations, stickers, receipts. Some cashiers are persistent. Learn to decline firmly but politely, even when they keep pushing.

### Setting
You're at checkout. The cashier has finished scanning and now begins the upsell routine. You want to pay and leave, but they have other plans.

---

## Complication 1: The Persistent Loyalty Card Push

**You said no, but they're not giving up.**

**Cashier:** Karta Moja Klubowa? `(KAR-ta MO-ya kloo-BO-va)` - Club card?

**You:** Nie mam. `(nyeh mam)` - I don't have one.

**Cashier:** Może pan/pani zrobić? To darmowe! `(MO-zheh pan/PA-nee ZRO-beech? to dar-MO-veh)` - Maybe you want to make one? It's free!

**Your options:**

**Option A - Firm decline:**
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.

**If they push again:**
- **You:** Naprawdę nie. `(na-PRAV-deh nyeh)` - Really no.
- **You:** Nie jestem zainteresowany/a. `(nyeh YES-tem za-een-te-re-SO-va-nih/a)` - I'm not interested. *(m/f)*

**Option B - Give a reason:**
- **You:** Nie mieszkam tutaj. `(nyeh MYESH-kam TOO-tay)` - I don't live here.
- **You:** Rzadko tu kupuję. `(ZHA-dko too koo-POO-yeh)` - I rarely shop here.

**Option C - "Maybe later" (to end it):**
- **You:** Może innym razem. `(MO-zheh EEN-nim RA-zem)` - Maybe another time.

---

## Complication 2: The App Download Pressure

**Now they want you to download the store app.**

**Cashier:** Ma pan/pani aplikację? `(ma pan/PA-nee ap-lee-KA-tsyeh)` - Do you have the app?
**Cashier:** Aplikacja Auchan? Lidl Plus? `(ap-lee-KA-tsya...)` - Auchan app? Lidl Plus?

**You:** Nie. `(nyeh)` - No.

**Cashier:** Może pan pobrać. Są kupony! `(MO-zheh pan po-BRACH. song koo-PO-nih)` - You can download it. There are coupons!

**Your options:**

**Option A - Decline:**
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.

**Option B - Excuse:**
- **You:** Nie mam miejsca w telefonie. `(nyeh mam MYEY-stsa v te-le-FO-nyeh)` - I don't have space on my phone.

**Option C - "Later":**
- **You:** Może w domu. `(MO-zheh v DO-moo)` - Maybe at home.

---

## Complication 3: Multiple Upsells in Sequence

**Card, app, charity, stickers - all at once.**

**Cashier:** Karta? `(KAR-ta)` - Card?
**You:** Nie. `(nyeh)` - No.

**Cashier:** Aplikacja? `(ap-lee-KA-tsya)` - App?
**You:** Nie. `(nyeh)` - No.

**Cashier:** Złotówka na WOŚP? `(zwo-TOOF-ka na voshp)` - One złoty for charity?
**You:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.

**Cashier:** Naklejki? `(na-KLEY-kee)` - Stickers?
**You:** Nie. `(nyeh)` - No.

**Survival tip:** Just keep saying "Nie" or "Nie, dziękuję" calmly. They have to ask - it's not personal.

---

## Complication 4: The Sticker Promotion

**They're collecting stickers for some promotion you don't understand.**

**Cashier:** Czy zbiera pan/pani naklejki? `(chih ZBYEH-ra pan/PA-nee na-KLEY-kee)` - Are you collecting stickers?

**You don't understand. Options:**

**Option A - Just say no:**
- **You:** Nie. `(nyeh)` - No.

**Option B - Ask what it is:**
- **You:** Przepraszam, co to jest? `(psheh-PRA-sham, tso to yest)` - Sorry, what is that?
- **Cashier:** *Explains rapidly in Polish*
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.

**If they give you stickers anyway:**
- Just take them or leave them - it doesn't matter

## Complication 5: Charity Donation Guilt

**They ask for charity and you feel awkward saying no.**

**Cashier:** Złotówka dla dzieci? `(zwo-TOOF-ka dla JEH-chee)` - One złoty for children?
**Cashier:** Może pan/pani zaokrąglić? `(MO-zheh pan/PA-nee za-ok-RONG-gleech)` - Maybe you can round up?

**Your options:**

**Option A - Donate:**
- **You:** Tak, dobrze. `(tak, DOB-zheh)` - Yes, okay.

**Option B - Decline:**
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.
- *This is perfectly acceptable*

**Option C - Not today:**
- **You:** Nie dzisiaj. `(nyeh JEE-shay)` - Not today.

---

## Complication 6: You Didn't Understand the Question

**Cashier asked something and you have no idea what.**

**Cashier:** [Rapid Polish question about some promotion]

**Your survival responses:**

**Option A - Ask for repeat:**
- **You:** Przepraszam? `(psheh-PRA-sham)` - Sorry?

**Option B - Just say no (safe default):**
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.
- *If it was an upsell, you declined. If it wasn't, they'll clarify.*

**Option C - Admit you don't understand:**
- **You:** Nie rozumiem. `(nyeh ro-ZOO-myem)` - I don't understand.

---

## Complication 7: They're Annoyed You Keep Saying No

**Some cashiers seem irritated after multiple declines.**

**What to do:**
- Don't apologize for declining - it's your right
- Stay polite but firm
- "Nie, dziękuję" is enough

**If they seem rude:**
- Ignore it
- Complete transaction
- Say "Do widzenia" and leave

## Cultural Notes

- **Required to ask**: Cashiers are often required to ask about cards, apps, and promotions. It's not personal.
- **"Nie, dziękuję" is complete**: You don't owe anyone an explanation.
- **Don't feel guilty**: Declining charity at checkout doesn't make you a bad person.
- **Stickers are for promotions**: Various stores run "collect stickers, get prizes" campaigns. Safe to decline if you don't understand.
- **Speed helps**: The faster you respond with "Nie," the fewer follow-ups you get.

## Practice Variations

### Intermediate Version
- Card offer: "Nie mam"
- App offer: "Nie"
- Charity: "Nie, dziękuję"

### Advanced Version
- Give reasons: "Nie mieszkam tutaj"
- Strong decline: "Naprawdę nie jestem zainteresowany"
- Ask what it is: "Przepraszam, co to jest?"
