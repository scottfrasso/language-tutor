## Scenario: Politely Declining Loyalty Card

### Your Goal
At checkout, the cashier offers you an Auchan loyalty card. You want to politely decline.

### Setting
You're at the Auchan checkout. The cashier is scanning your items and will ask about the loyalty card.

---

## Dialogue

### Opening

**Cashier:** Dzień dobry! `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day!

**You:** Dzień dobry. `(jen DOB-rih)` - Good day.

*Cashier scans your items*

---

### Loyalty Card Offer

**Cashier:** Karta Moja Klubowa? `(KAR-ta MO-ya kloo-BO-va)` - Your club card?

---

### Ways to Decline

**Option A - Simple:**
- **You:** Nie mam. `(nyeh mam)` - I don't have [one].
- **Cashier:** Może pan/pani chce zrobić? To darmowe. `(MO-zheh pan/PA-nee htse ZRO-beech? to dar-MO-veh)` - Maybe you want to make one? It's free.

**Option B - Polite decline:**
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh)` - No, thank you.
- **Cashier:** *Continues with transaction*

**Option C - More explanation:**
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. Nie mieszkam tutaj. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh. nyeh MYESH-kam TOO-tay)` - No, thank you. I don't live here.
- **Cashier:** Rozumiem. `(ro-ZOO-myem)` - I understand.

**Option D - Asking about it:**
- **You:** Co to jest? `(tso to yest)` - What is it?
- **Cashier:** Karta rabatowa. Zniżki na zakupy. `(KAR-ta ra-ba-TO-va. ZNEEZH-kee na za-KOO-pih)` - Discount card. Discounts on shopping.
- **You:** Nie, dziękuję. Może innym razem. `(nyeh, jen-KOO-yeh. MO-zheh EEN-nim RA-zem)` - No, thank you. Maybe another time.

---

### Payment

**Cashier:** Dwadzieścia pięć złotych. `(dva-JESH-cha pyench ZWO-tih)` - Twenty-five złoty.

**You:** Proszę. `(PRO-sheh)` - Here you go.

---

### Closing

**Cashier:** Dziękuję, do widzenia. `(jen-KOO-yeh, do vee-JEN-ya)` - Thank you, goodbye.

**You:** Do widzenia. `(do vee-JEN-ya)` - Goodbye.

## Cultural Notes

- **Loyalty cards are everywhere**: Most Polish stores have loyalty programs (Payback, Moja Klubowa, etc.)
- **Cashiers must ask**: Cashiers are often required to ask about loyalty cards - it's not personal!
- **"Nie, dziękuję" is enough**: You don't need to explain. A simple "No, thank you" is perfectly polite.
- **They might persist**: Some cashiers are trained to offer once more. A second "Nie, dziękuję" is fine.
- **Free to join**: Most loyalty cards are free and offer real discounts, so locals often have them.

## Other Common Upsells

**Receipt for warranty:**
- **Cashier:** Paragon na gwarancję? `(pa-RA-gon na gva-RAN-tsyeh)` - Receipt for warranty?
- **You:** Tak, poproszę. `(tak, po-PRO-sheh)` - Yes, please. *(if buying electronics)*

**Plastic bag:**
- **Cashier:** Torba? `(TOR-ba)` - Bag?
- **You:** Tak / Nie, dziękuję.

**Charity donation:**
- **Cashier:** Złotówka na cel charytatywny? `(zwo-TOOF-ka na tsel ha-rih-ta-TIV-nih)` - One złoty for charity?
- **You:** Tak / Nie, dziękuję.

## Practice Variations

### Intermediate Version
- When asked: "Nie mam"
- If they persist: "Nie, dziękuję"

### Advanced Version
- Decline politely with reason: "Nie, dziękuję. Rzadko tu kupuję" (No thanks, I rarely shop here)
- If you change your mind: "Dobrze, proszę zrobić kartę" (Okay, please make a card)
