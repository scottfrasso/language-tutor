## Scenario: Asking for Recommendations at Auchan

### Your Goal
You're looking at two different brands of yogurt and want to ask a store employee which one is better or which they recommend.

### Setting
You're in the dairy section at Auchan, holding two different yogurt brands, looking uncertain. A store employee is nearby.

---

## Dialogue

### Getting Attention

**You:** Przepraszam. `(psheh-PRA-sham)` - Excuse me.

**Employee:** Tak? Słucham? `(tak? SWOO-ham)` - Yes? How can I help?

---

### Asking for Recommendation

**You:** Który jogurt jest lepszy? `(KTOO-rih YO-goort yest LEP-shih)` - Which yogurt is better?

**Alternative phrasings:**
- **You:** Który pan/pani poleca? `(KTOO-rih pan/PA-nee po-LEH-tsa)` - Which do you recommend?
- **You:** Co jest lepsze? Ten czy ten? `(tso yest LEP-sheh? ten chih ten)` - What's better? This one or this one?

---

### Employee's Responses

**Response A - Recommends one:**
- **Employee:** Ten jest bardzo dobry. Ja zawsze kupuję ten. `(ten yest BAR-dzo DOB-rih. ya ZAV-sheh koo-POO-yeh ten)` - This one is very good. I always buy this one.
- **You:** Dobrze, dziękuję. Wezmę ten. `(DOB-zheh, jen-KOO-yeh. VEZ-meh ten)` - Okay, thank you. I'll take this one.

**Response B - Asks about preference:**
- **Employee:** Co pan/pani lubi? Gęsty czy lekki? `(tso pan/PA-nee LOO-bee? GENS-tih chih LEK-kee)` - What do you like? Thick or light?
- **You:** Gęsty. `(GENS-tih)` - Thick.
- **Employee:** To ten. `(to ten)` - Then this one.

**Response C - Price difference:**
- **Employee:** Ten jest tańszy, ale ten jest lepszej jakości. `(ten yest TAN-shih, ALE ten yest LEP-shey ya-KOSH-chee)` - This one is cheaper, but this one is better quality.
- **You:** Wezmę ten lepszy. `(VEZ-meh ten LEP-shih)` - I'll take the better one.

---

### Following Up

**You:** Dziękuję za pomoc. `(jen-KOO-yeh za PO-mots)` - Thank you for the help.

**Employee:** Proszę bardzo! `(PRO-sheh BAR-dzo)` - You're very welcome!

## Cultural Notes

- **Employees are usually helpful**: Polish store employees, especially in hypermarkets, are generally willing to give recommendations.
- **Price vs Quality**: Poles are practical shoppers and often discuss price vs quality trade-offs.
- **Personal preference matters**: Don't be afraid to specify what you like (gęsty/thick, lekki/light, słodki/sweet, etc.)

---

## Practice Variations

### Intermediate Version
- "Który jest lepszy?"
- Listen and accept recommendation
- "Dziękuję"

### Advanced Version
- Give context: "Szukam dobrego jogurtu" (I'm looking for good yogurt)
- Ask specific questions: "Który jest najświeższy?" (Which is freshest?)
- Follow up: "Dziękuję za pomoc!" (Thanks for the help!)
